Hello creative inventors and crackpot scientists!

AnomalyCon has officially settled on the details of our first annual Steampunk Lego(R) contest!

Competitions will be split up into two categories with two age groups (under 13 and 13+)in each category:

Category 1: Plotted and Planned
Those who bring their own pre-built invention using their blocks. Entry fee is $2. Inventors are competing for prizes and the illustrious Golden Cog award (pictures of the finished AnomalyCon trophy coming soon!)
The weight limit for bring-your-own-inventions is 2lb.

Category 2: Time Challenge
Those who build their invention on site utilizing our blocks. Entry fee is $5 to accommodate the generous lending of a great Lego collection for this event. Inventors are competing again for prizes and the illustrious Golden Cog.

One Golden Cog will be awarded for each category/age group with at least 3 entrants. Fabulous Lego prizes will be awarded!

Our judges will be Chris Martinez of Necro Construct, London Homer-Wambeam (a reigning Lego champion), and a surprise third judge–just to make sure no bribes occur.

The contest itself will take place from 9AM-2PM on Sunday, March 25th. Sign-up sheets will be available at the Operations table at the con all weekend. See you all there!

For more information, use the contact form.

We are excited to announce new Steampunk films that will be screened at AnomalyCon 2012!

First, London Homer-Wambeam (the director of last year’s film, Forewarned) is releasing a new feature film, The Astronomer. This production is a remake of George Méliès’ 1902 film Le Voyage dans la Lune (A Trip to the Moon).
Watch the Trailer.

Second, Mike Zawacki is releasing The Wars of Other Men. The film–which has dieselpunk tones–is set in the once-powerful Detroit, which has become both battleground and tactical objective in a long-running conflict.
Watch the Trailer.

If you’re interested in having your movie, short film, Steampunk music video or documentary screened at AnomalyCon 2012 or 2013, contact us using the contact form!
